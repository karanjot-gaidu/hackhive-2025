Chapter 5: JUPITER
5.1 Physical Properties and Orbital Dynamics
5.1.1 Basic Physical Properties
Mass: 1.8982 × 10²⁷ kg
Volume: 1.4313 × 10¹⁵ km³
Equatorial radius: 71,492 km
Polar radius: 66,854 km
Surface gravity: 24.79 m/s²
Escape velocity: 59.5 km/s
Moment of inertia factor: 0.254
Surface pressure: Not applicable (gas giant)
5.1.2 Orbital Characteristics
Semi-major axis: 778,570,000 km
Orbital period: 4,332.59 days
Average orbital speed: 13.07 km/s
Mean anomaly: 20.020°
Orbital inclination: 1.303°
Longitude of ascending node: 100.464°
Argument of perihelion: 273.867°
5.2 Atmospheric Science
5.2.1 Composition
Hydrogen (H₂): 89.8%
Helium (He): 10.2%
Methane (CH₄): 0.3%
Ammonia (NH₃): 0.026%
Hydrogen deuteride (HD): 0.003%
Ethane (C₂H₆): 0.0006%
5.2.2 Atmospheric Structure
Troposphere (0-50 km): Clouds, bands, Great Red Spot
Stratosphere (50-320 km): Temperature inversions
Thermosphere (320-1000 km): Aurora activity
Exosphere (>1000 km): Transition to space
5.3 Surface Features
5.3.1 Major Features
Great Red Spot: Persistent storm system (ongoing for centuries)
Bands and Zones: Distinctive atmospheric patterns
Polar Auroras: Intense magnetic field interactions
Impact Basins: Temporary dark spots from impact events
5.3.2 Impact Features
Crater distribution in atmospheric patterns
Evidence of past impact events (Shoemaker-Levy 9)
5.4 Internal Structure
5.4.1 Core
Radius: ~7,000 km
State: Rocky/Metallic
Magnetic field: Strongest in solar system
Thickness: ~7,000 km
Composition: Iron and silicates
5.4.2 Mantle
State: Liquid metallic
Magnetic field: Strong convection currents
Thickness: ~40,000 km
Composition: Metallic hydrogen
5.4.3 Outer Layer
State: Fluid transitioning to gas
Magnetic field: Complex interactions
Thickness: ~20,000 km
Composition: Molecular hydrogen and helium
