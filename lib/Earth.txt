Chapter 3: EARTH
3.1 Physical Properties and Orbital Dynamics
3.1.1 Basic Physical Properties
Mass: 5.9724 × 10²⁴ kg
Volume: 1.08321 × 10¹² km³
Equatorial radius: 6,378.137 km
Polar radius: 6,356.752 km
Surface gravity: 9.80665 m/s²
Escape velocity: 11.186 km/s
Moment of inertia factor: 0.3307
Surface pressure: 101.325 kPa
3.1.2 Orbital Characteristics
Semi-major axis: 149,598,023 km
Orbital period: 365.256 days
Average orbital speed: 29.78 km/s
Mean anomaly: 358.617°
Orbital inclination: 0.00005°
Longitude of ascending node: 348.73936°
Argument of perihelion: 114.20783°
3.2 Atmospheric Science
3.2.1 Composition
Nitrogen (N₂): 78.084%
Oxygen (O₂): 20.946%
Argon (Ar): 0.934%
Carbon dioxide (CO₂): 0.0417%
Neon (Ne): 0.001818%
Water vapor (H₂O): Variable (0-4%)
3.2.2 Atmospheric Structure
Troposphere (0-12 km): Weather systems, temperature decrease
Stratosphere (12-50 km): Ozone layer, temperature increase
Mesosphere (50-80 km): Coldest layer, meteor burnup
Thermosphere (80-700 km): Aurora, ionization effects
3.3 Surface Features
3.3.1 Major Terrain Types
Oceans: Cover 70.8% of surface
Continents: Seven major tectonic plates
Mountain Ranges: Fold and fault formations
Impact Basins: Preserved ancient craters
3.3.2 Impact Features
Crater distribution affected by erosion
Evidence of major extinction events
3.4 Internal Structure
3.4.1 Core
Radius: ~3,485 km
State: Liquid outer, solid inner
Magnetic field: Strong dipolar
Thickness: ~3,485 km

3.4.2 Mantle
State: Solid silicate
Magnetic field: Contributes to dynamo
Thickness: ~2,890 km

State: Solid, two types (oceanic and continental)
Magnetic field: Local variations
Thickness: 5-70 km
Composition: Granitic and basaltic rocks
