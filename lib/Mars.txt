Chapter 1: MARS
1.1 Physical Properties and Orbital Dynamics
1.1.1 Basic Physical Properties
Mass: 6.4171 × 10²³ kg
Volume: 1.6318 × 10¹¹ km³
Equatorial radius: 3,389.5 km
Polar radius: 3,376.2 km
Surface gravity: 3.71 m/s²
Escape velocity: 5.03 km/s
Moment of inertia factor: 0.366
Surface pressure: ~0.636 kPa
1.1.2 Orbital Characteristics
Semi-major axis: 227,939,200 km
Orbital period: 686.98 days
Average orbital speed: 24.077 km/s
Mean anomaly: 19.412°
Orbital inclination: 1.850°
Longitude of ascending node: 49.558°
Argument of perihelion: 286.502°
1.2 Atmospheric Science
1.2.1 Composition
Carbon dioxide (CO₂): 95.3%
Nitrogen (N₂): 2.7%
Argon (Ar): 1.6%
Oxygen (O₂): 0.13%
Carbon monoxide (CO): 0.08%
Water vapor (H₂O): 0.03%
1.2.2 Atmospheric Structure
Troposphere (0-40 km): Thin and dynamic, major dust storms
Mesosphere (40-100 km): Temperature inversions, CO₂ condensation
Thermosphere (100-200 km): Aurorae, ionization effects
Exosphere (>200 km): Gradual transition to space
1.3 Surface Features
1.3.1 Major Terrain Types
Volcanic Plateaus: Olympus Mons (largest volcano in the Solar System)
Canyons: Valles Marineris (spans over 4,000 km)
Plains: Vastitas Borealis (lowland region, possible ancient ocean bed)
Impact Basins: Hellas Planitia (largest impact structure on Mars)
1.3.2 Impact Features
Crater distribution linked to Mars’ geological history
Evidence of ancient water activity in some craters
1.4 Internal Structure
1.4.1 Core
Radius: ~1,800 km
State: Partially liquid
Composition: Iron, sulfur, nickel
Magnetic field: Weak, localized remnants
1.4.2 Mantle
Thickness: ~1,700 km
Composition: Silicate rock, convection-driven
1.4.3 Crust
Thickness: 20-50 km
Composition: Basaltic, signs of past tectonic activity
